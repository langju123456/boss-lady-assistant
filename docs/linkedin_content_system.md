# LinkedIn Content System

## Purpose

This project is being built publicly.

The documentation should automatically become future content.

The founder should never need to manually think:

"What should I post today?"

Instead:

```text
Lessons Learned
-> LinkedIn Post

Customer Discovery
-> LinkedIn Post

Product Pivot
-> LinkedIn Post

Architecture Decision
-> LinkedIn Post
```

The source of truth is:

```text
docs/06_lessons_learned.md
```

Over time, this repository should accumulate reusable material for:

* LinkedIn
* Product case studies
* PM interviews
* AI PM portfolio
* Founder storytelling
* Future blog posts

Every lesson learned should become reusable content.

## Content Pipeline

```text
Real observation
-> Lesson learned
-> Product decision
-> Content angle
-> LinkedIn post
-> Portfolio / case study material
```

## Content Type 1: Product Thinking

Examples:

* Why customers don't buy AI
* Why the first version should be simple
* Why dashboards are not always the answer

Template:

```text
Insight
↓
Original Assumption
↓
What Changed
↓
Decision
↓
Broader Lesson
```

Best used for:

* Product pivots
* MVP scope decisions
* User behavior insights
* Product management reflections

## Content Type 2: Customer Discovery

Examples:

* Talking to salon owners
* Observing workflows
* Discovering pain points

Template:

```text
What I Expected
↓
What I Observed
↓
What Surprised Me
↓
How The Product Changed
```

Best used for:

* Interview notes
* Real user quotes
* Workflow observations
* Pain point discovery

## Content Type 3: Startup Journey

Examples:

* Product pivots
* MVP decisions
* Early mistakes

Template:

```text
What I Thought
↓
What Happened
↓
What I Learned
↓
Next Step
```

Best used for:

* Founder reflections
* Build-in-public updates
* Early-stage uncertainty
* Decision-making under limited information

## Content Type 4: AI Product Development

Examples:

* Agent architecture
* Tool calling
* Building for real users
* Why RAG was postponed

Template:

```text
Technical Assumption
↓
Reality
↓
Product Impact
↓
New Direction
```

Best used for:

* Technical architecture decisions
* AI agent design
* Model/tooling tradeoffs
* Choosing simple systems over impressive systems

## LinkedIn Writing Style

Style:

* Founder style
* Product Manager style
* AI Builder style

Avoid:

* Hype
* Buzzwords
* Fake success claims
* Fake metrics

Write honestly.

Focus on:

* Learning
* Product thinking
* Customer discovery
* Decision making

## Content Creation Rule

Whenever a new lesson is added to `docs/06_lessons_learned.md`, generate:

1. 1 long LinkedIn post, 500-800 words
2. 1 medium LinkedIn post, 200-400 words
3. 3 short LinkedIn posts, 100-200 words each
4. 1 hook list with 10 hooks
5. 1 founder reflection version
6. 1 AI Product Manager version

## Example Input

Lesson:

Original Assumption:
AI is the product.

New Insight:
AI is the tool.

Customers buy outcomes.

Product Decision:
Focus on business value instead of AI features.

## Example Output Title Ideas

* Customers Don't Buy AI
* The Biggest Mistake I Made Building My First AI Product
* Why I Stopped Thinking About Models
* AI Is Not The Product
* What Salon Owners Taught Me About Product Management

## Reuse Strategy

One lesson can become multiple assets:

* LinkedIn post
* Founder reflection
* Product case study paragraph
* AI PM portfolio story
* Interview answer
* Blog outline
* Future pitch narrative

The same truth can be repackaged, but the core lesson should remain honest and grounded in actual project experience.
